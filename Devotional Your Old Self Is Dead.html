Devotional Study: "Your Old Self Is Dead"

Primary Scripture: Romans 6:6-14"For we know that our old self was crucified with him so that the body ruled by sin might be done away with, that we should no longer be slaves to sin... For sin shall no longer be your master, because you are not under the law, but under grace." (NIV)

Introduction: The Neuroscience of New Creation

Both Scripture and modern psychology confirm: we become what we declare. fMRI studies show that verbal declarations activate the prefrontal cortex, strengthening our ability to resist destructive patterns (University of Texas, 2020). When Paul says "old self" is dead, he's revealing a neurological reality before it's a spiritual one.

Research Insight: A 2019 Harvard study found that participants who affirmed "I am the type of person who..." statements showed 32% greater resistance to temptation than control groups (Psychological Science).

1. The Death Certificate

A. Psychological Reality

  • "Neuroplasticity" proves we can rewire identity (Doidge, 2007)
  • Cognitive Behavioral Therapy shows that changing self-talk alters behavior (Beck, 1979)
  • The "Self-Perception Theory" (Bem, 1972) demonstrates we believe what we declare

B. Biblical Foundation

  • 2 Corinthians 5:17"Therefore, if anyone is in Christ, the new creation has come: The old has gone, the new is here!" - Present reality
  • Galatians 2:20"I have been crucified with Christ and I no longer live, but Christ lives in me." - Past-tense death
  • Colossians 3:3"For you died, and your life is now hidden with Christ in God." - Secure identity
Daily Declaration: "I am a new creation in Christ (2 Corinthians 5:17"Therefore, if anyone is in Christ, the new creation has come: The old has gone, the new is here!"). The power of sin over me was broken at the cross (Romans 6:6"For we know that our old self was crucified with him so that the body ruled by sin might be done away with..."). 2. The Resurrection Lifestyle

A. Practical Neurotheology

  • Verbal declarations create new neural pathways (UCLA, 2018)
  • 90 seconds of focused breathing while declaring truth reduces amygdala activation (Harvard Medical School)
  • The "Implementation Intention" strategy (Gollwitzer, 1999) proves "If-Then" planning increases self-control by 200%

B. Biblical Strategies

  • Ephesians 4:22-24"You were taught... to put off your old self... to be made new in the attitude of your minds; and to put on the new self..." - The "put off/put on" principle
  • James 4:7"Submit yourselves, then, to God. Resist the devil, and he will flee from you." - The submission-resistance sequence
  • 1 Corinthians 10:13"No temptation has overtaken you except what is common to mankind..." - The way of escape
Resurrection Practice:
1. When tempted, declare: "This isn't who I am - I died to that!"
2. Breathe deeply for 90 seconds while repeating Romans 6:11"In the same way, count yourselves dead to sin but alive to God in Christ Jesus."
3. Replace the temptation with a "put on" behavior (prayer, Scripture, worship)

3. The Grave Clothes Dilemma

A. Why We Still Feel Dead

  • Neural Pathways: Old habits have "well-worn" brain trails
  • Emotional Memory: The amygdala remembers past failures
  • Community Reinforcement: Others may still see us through old identity

B. Biblical Solutions

  • Romans 12:2"Do not conform to the pattern of this world, but be transformed by the renewing of your mind." - Continuous renewal
  • Philippians 3:13-14"Forgetting what is behind and straining toward what is ahead..." - Forward focus
  • 1 Peter 2:9"But you are a chosen people... belonging to God..." - Identity reminders
Identity Prayer:
"Father, I receive today that my old self was crucified with Christ. I tear off these grave clothes of past identity. Flood my neural pathways with the reality of my new nature. When temptation comes, quicken me to declare who I truly am in Jesus. Amen."
