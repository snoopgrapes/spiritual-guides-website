Trust is Rebuilt Through Consistent Faithfulness

A study on how reliability in small things leads to restored trust, based on Proverbs 25:19 and Luke 16:10.

1. The Consequences of Broken Trust

"Like a broken tooth or a lame foot is reliance on the unfaithful in a time of trouble."
— Proverbs 25:19 (NIV)

A broken tooth or lame foot makes basic functions painful or impossible. Similarly, trusting someone unfaithful leads to disappointment and hardship. When trust is broken, reliance on that person becomes unreliable and even harmful.

Have you ever experienced the pain of broken trust? How did it affect your relationship?

2. Faithfulness in Small Things Builds Trust

"Whoever can be trusted with very little can also be trusted with much, and whoever is dishonest with very little will also be dishonest with much."
— Luke 16:10 (NIV)

Key Truth: Trust is earned through consistency in small, everyday actions. If someone proves faithful in minor matters, they can be trusted with greater responsibilities.

Supporting Scriptures:
• Matthew 25:21 - "Well done, good and faithful servant!"
• Proverbs 28:20 - "A faithful person will be richly blessed..."

How can you practice faithfulness in small things today (e.g., keeping promises, punctuality, honesty)?

3. Practical Steps to Rebuild Trust

  1. Start Small – Demonstrate reliability in minor commitments.
  2. Be Consistent – Trust grows over time through repeated faithfulness.
  3. Repent & Restore – If you've broken trust, acknowledge it and commit to change (James 5:16).
  4. Be Patient – Rebuilding trust takes time (Ecclesiastes 7:8).

Final Thought: Just as God is always faithful (Lamentations 3:22-23), we should strive to reflect His faithfulness in our relationships.

"Lord, help me to be trustworthy in small things so that I may honor You and rebuild broken trust. Teach me to walk in faithfulness as You do. Amen."
