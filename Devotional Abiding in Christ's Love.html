üîç Focus

How does abiding in Christ's love change my relationships?

Jesus reveals a love cascade: The Father loves the Son ‚Üí The Son loves us ‚Üí We abide in that love ‚Üí That love flows through us to others. This divine love (Greek: agapƒì) differs from human affection:

  • Source: We love from Christ's love, not for it (1 John 4:19)
  • Quality: His love is sacrificial (John 15:13), unconditional (Romans 5:8), and eternal (Jeremiah 31:3)
  • Effect: Abiding love transforms how we view and treat people:
"We love others best when we're most satisfied in God's love for us."
- John Piper

Relational transformations:

  1. Patience: Secure in Christ's love, we don't demand others meet our needs
  2. Forgiveness: Loved despite our flaws, we extend grace more freely
  3. Boundaries: Filled with divine love, we don't cling to unhealthy relationships
  4. Evangelism: Overflowing love naturally shares the Source

‚úã Practice

Love someone today with Christ's love flowing through you.

How to practice channeled love:

  1. Select one person (easy or challenging)
  2. Pray: "Jesus, love [name] through me in the way they most need today"
  3. Act on the first prompting you sense (call, serve, encourage, forgive)
  4. Reflect afterward: How did this differ from human-powered love?

Examples:

  • Text an encouragement you'd want to receive
  • Buy coffee for a coworker without announcing it
  • Listen without interrupting to a family member
