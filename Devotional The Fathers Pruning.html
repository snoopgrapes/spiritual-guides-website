John 15:1-2 (ESV)

"I am the true vine, and my Father is the vinedresser. Every branch in me that does not bear fruit he takes away, and every branch that does bear fruit he prunes, that it may bear more fruit."

üîç Focus

What might God be pruning in my life right now?

Vinedressers prune for two purposes: removing dead growth and shaping living branches for maximum fruitfulness. Consider these areas:

  • Relationships: Has God removed certain connections to redirect your growth?
  • Opportunities: Have doors closed to focus your energy elsewhere?
  • Habits: What behaviors is the Spirit convicting you to cut away?
  • Self-sufficiency: Is God pruning your independence to deepen reliance on Him?
  • Good things: Even fruitful areas may be trimmed for greater yield later
"Pruning isn't punishment - it's promotion. The vinedresser never wastes a cut."
- Anonymous vineyard keeper

The Greek word for "prunes" (kathair≈ç) means to cleanse or purify. God's pruning always has purpose.

‚úã Practice

Thank God for a current difficulty, trusting it's for your growth.

How to practice redemptive gratitude:

  1. Identify one current challenge (health, relationships, work, etc.)
  2. Reframe it as potential pruning: "Father, is this Your careful cut?"
  3. Thank Him specifically for how this could produce growth (even if unseen now)
  4. Journal the prayer and revisit it weekly to trace God's work

Example: "Thank You for this financial pressure, trusting You're pruning my dependence on material security and growing my faith in Your provision."
