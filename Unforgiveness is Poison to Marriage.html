1. The Command to Forgive

Colossians 3:12-14 - "Therefore, as God's chosen people, holy and dearly loved, clothe yourselves with compassion, kindness, humility, gentleness and patience. Bear with each other and forgive one another if any of you has a grievance against someone. Forgive as the Lord forgave you. And over all these virtues put on love, which binds them all together in perfect unity."

Discussion Points:

  • Forgiveness is a choice, not just a feeling. We are commanded to "clothe ourselves" with virtues like compassion and forgiveness.
  • Marriage requires bearing with one another. No spouse is perfect; patience and humility are essential.
  • Love binds everything together. Without forgiveness, love cannot thrive, and unity is broken.

Reflection Question:

Is there an unresolved offense in your marriage that you need to release to God?

2. The Extent of Forgiveness

Matthew 18:21-22 - "Then Peter came to Jesus and asked, 'Lord, how many times shall I forgive my brother or sister who sins against me? Up to seven times?' Jesus answered, 'I tell you, not seven times, but seventy-seven times.'"

Discussion Points:

  • Forgiveness has no limits. Jesus calls us to forgive repeatedly, just as God forgives us (Ephesians 4:32).
  • Unforgiveness leads to bitterness. Hebrews 12:15 warns that a "root of bitterness" can defile a marriage.
  • Forgiveness is a reflection of God's grace. Just as we are forgiven much, we must forgive much (Luke 7:47).

Reflection Question:

Are you keeping a record of wrongs in your marriage? How can you choose to release it today?
