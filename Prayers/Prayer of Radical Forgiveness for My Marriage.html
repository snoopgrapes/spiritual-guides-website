Prayer of Radical Forgiveness for My Marriage

(Based on Colossians 3:12–14 & Matthew 18:21–22)

Gracious Father,

Your Word calls me to 'clothe myself with compassion, kindness, humility, gentleness, and patience' (Colossians 3:12). But I confess there are times when hurt has hardened my heart. Where I've held onto resentment toward my wife—where I've kept score of her mistakes or withheld grace—forgive me. Break the chains of bitterness, and teach me to forgive as freely as You have forgiven me.

Jesus, You said to forgive 'not seven times, but seventy-seven times' (Matthew 18:22). So today, I choose to release my wife completely—for the words that wounded, the love that felt lacking, and the disappointments that linger. I let go of every 'record of wrongs' (1 Corinthians 13:5) and trust You to heal what's been broken between us.

Fill me with Your Spirit so I may 'bear with [her] in love' (Colossians 3:13). When my flesh whispers, 'This hurt is too deep,' remind me of the Cross—where Your mercy proved no sin is beyond redemption. Knit our hearts together in 'perfect unity' (v. 14), and make our marriage a testimony of Your restoring power.

Above all, let love be the bond that holds us together (Colossians 3:14). Where I've failed to love well, renew me. Where we've drifted apart, draw us close again. May our relationship reflect the covenant love You have for Your people—patient, unselfish, and enduring.

In the name of Jesus, the healer of all wounds, Amen.

Scripture references: Colossians 3:12-14, Matthew 18:21-22, 1 Corinthians 13:5
