Breath of Life Prayer for Our Marriage

(A Husband's Cry for Divine Regeneration)

Creator God,

You who breathed life into dust at creation, breathe now upon the dry bones of our marriage (Ezekiel 37:5). Where we built on the shifting sand of human strength—selfish desires, worldly expectations, fleshly passions—raise up a new covenant built upon Christ, the solid Rock.

As You promised to 'restore the years the locusts have eaten' (Joel 2:25), do more than repair—resurrect. Not a patched-up version of what was, but a holy regeneration where:

What was flesh-driven becomes Spirit-led
What was conditional becomes covenantal
What was two lives parallel becomes one flesh in You

Lord of the Impossible (Jeremiah 32:27):

Turn these bones of dead affection into living love

Replace our heart of stone with hearts of flesh (Ezekiel 36:26)

Make our marriage a valley of dry bones no more, but an army of resurrection testimony

Let every restored moment—every healed wound, every renewed vow—declare: 'This is the Lord's doing' (Psalm 118:23). No longer bound by our past failures, but freed by Your promise: 'Behold, I make all things new' (Revelation 21:5).

Come, Breath of Heaven. Breathe upon us as we kneel together on this fresh foundation—Christ alone.

In the name of the Resurrection and the Life, Amen.

Scripture references: Ezekiel 37:5, Joel 2:25, Jeremiah 32:27,
Ezekiel 36:26, Psalm 118:23, Revelation 21:5
