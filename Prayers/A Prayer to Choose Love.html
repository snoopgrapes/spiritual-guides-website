A Prayer to Choose Love

(For My Wife in Our Broken Season)

Heavenly Father,

Your Word defines love not as a feeling, but as a choice—patient, kind, and selfless (1 Corinthians 13:4–5). Today, I choose to love my wife on purpose, even when it's hard. Forgive me for the times I've made love conditional—waiting for her to change first, or withholding affection because of hurt. Break my pride and teach me to love like Jesus, who laid down His life for us (John 15:13).

Where our marriage feels fractured, help me to:

Be patient (1 Corinthians 13:4)—to listen without interrupting, to wait without demanding.
Act kindly—to speak life, not criticism; to serve, not keep score.
Let go of resentment—to 'keep no record of wrongs' (v. 5), just as You've erased mine.

Lord, love 'always protects, always trusts, always hopes, always perseveres' (v. 7). So I ask for the strength to protect our covenant—not because my wife deserves it, but because You call me to it. When I'm tempted to give up, remind me: Love is not a surrender to circumstances, but a surrender to You.

Jesus, You chose the cross before we chose You. Let that same sacrificial love flow through me—not in my power, but by Your Spirit. Rebuild what's broken between us, one intentional act of love at a time.

In Your unwavering name, Amen.

Scripture references: 1 Corinthians 13:4-7, John 15:13
