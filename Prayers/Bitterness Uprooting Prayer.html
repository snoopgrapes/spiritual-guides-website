A Biblical Prayer for Uprooting Bitterness

Heavenly Father,

I approach Your grace-filled throne with a humble heart. Thank You for Your unwavering love and fresh mercies each day.

Lord, I confess the root of bitterness within me. It has poisoned my spirit, defiled my thoughts, and hurt my relationships, especially with [mention the person or situation, e.g., "my wife," or "those who have wronged me"]. Forgive this sin, O God, for it opposes the peace and holiness You call me to.

I stand "under grace," knowing "sin will have no dominion over me." I cannot remove this bitterness alone; only Your transformative grace, given through Christ, can. By Your Holy Spirit, expose and extract every trace of this bitterness from my soul.

Replace this bitter root with Your spirit of gentleness, kindness, and compassion. Help me to forgive as You have forgiven me. Let me see [the person/situation] through Your merciful eyes, not through my hurt.

I renounce bitterness's hold on me. By Your grace, I release all pain, anger, and desire for retribution. Fill me with Your divine love that "bears all things, believes all things, hopes all things, endures all things" (1 Corinthians 13:7). Empower me to actively pursue peace with everyone and the holiness essential to seeing You.

Thank You, Father, for hearing me. I trust Your power to heal and renew me. May my life reflect the freedom and forgiveness found in Your Son.

In Jesus' mighty name,

Amen.
