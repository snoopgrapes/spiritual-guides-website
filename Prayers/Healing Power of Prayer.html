Healing Power of Prayer

Heavenly Father,

Your Word says that when two agree in prayer, You move powerfully (Matthew 18:19-20). Though we're not praying together yet, I come before You today asking for the miracle of restored unity. Prepare both our hearts for that sacred moment when we can approach You as one again.

Forgive me for the ways I've neglected this holy privilege of praying with my wife. Where pride, busyness, or hurt have kept us from joining in prayer, soften my heart first, Lord. Make me the husband who leads us back to You in humility, just as James teaches that confession and prayer bring healing (James 5:16).

As I pray alone today, I ask You to:

Prepare the Way - Quiet the storms between us so we can hear Your voice together
Give Me Courage - To initiate prayer without forcing or demanding
Mend Our Foundation - Rebuild what's been broken so when we do pray together, it's in truth and love

Lord, I claim Your promise that where just two gather in Your name, You are present (Matthew 18:20). Even now as I pray alone, plant seeds for that future moment when we'll pray together again. Make me patient in the waiting, faithful in praying, and tender in approaching my wife when the time is right.

I trust that no marriage is beyond Your healing when we return to You in prayer. Let our story become a testimony to Your restoring power.

In Jesus' name, Amen

Scripture references: Matthew 18:19-20, James 5:16
