A Husband's Prayer to Rebuild Trust

Heavenly Father,

Your Word warns that 'trusting in an unfaithful man is like a bad tooth or a foot out of joint' (Proverbs 25:19). I confess the ways I've broken trust in my marriage—through my words, actions, or failures. Forgive me, and teach me to be 'faithful in the little things' (Luke 16:10) so my wife can slowly believe in me again.

Lord, rebuild what I've damaged:

With my words: Let me speak only truth, even when it's hard
With my actions: Help me follow through on the smallest promises
With my time: Show me how to prioritize her heart over my distractions

Jesus, You are the perfect Bridegroom—faithful even when we are faithless. Mold me into that kind of husband. Give me patience to accept that trust grows through consistent small choices, not grand gestures. When discouragement comes, remind me: 'Whoever can be trusted with little can be trusted with much' (Luke 16:10).

I surrender my timeline for healing. Prepare my wife's heart as I prove my change is real—not just in words, but in daily, quiet faithfulness.

In Your restoring name, Amen.

Scripture references: Proverbs 25:19, Luke 16:10
