Prayer: The Nature of Offense

Lord Jesus,

You warned us that offenses would come, but You also showed us how to overcome them. "Woe to the world for temptations to sin! For it is necessary that temptations come, but woe to the one by whom the temptation comes!" (Matthew 18:7)

Forgive me for the times I've clung to offense as if it were my right, when in truth it only poisons my soul. "See to it that no one fails to obtain the grace of God; that no 'root of bitterness' springs up and causes trouble, and by it many become defiled." (Hebrews 12:15)

Reveal to me today any hidden offenses I'm carrying—those grudges I've nursed, those wounds I've rehearsed, those injustices I've catalogued. Shine Your light on these dark corners of my heart.

Help me understand that releasing offense isn't excusing wrong behavior—it's freeing myself from Satan's trap. "A person's wisdom yields patience; it is to one's glory to overlook an offense." (Proverbs 19:11)

Give me discernment to recognize when I'm beginning to take offense, and courage to release it immediately. Let me walk in the freedom of an unoffendable heart, secure in Your love.

In the name of Jesus, who endured the ultimate offense for my sake,
Amen.
