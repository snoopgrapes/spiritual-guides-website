A Husband's Prayer of Humility

Heavenly Father,

Your Word says You 'oppose the proud but give grace to the humble' (James 4:6). Today, I bow my heart before You and my wife. Forgive me for the times I've acted in pride—thinking of myself first, insisting on my own way, or failing to listen with patience. Strip away my selfishness and clothe me in the humility of Christ, who 'emptied Himself' for love (Philippians 2:7).

Lord, teach me to 'count my wife more significant than myself' (Philippians 2:3). Where I've been stubborn, critical, or defensive, soften my heart. Help me to 'submit to You' (James 4:7) so I may serve her with gentleness, not selfish ambition. When disagreements arise, remind me that unity is worth more than winning an argument.

Break any pride that has wounded our marriage. I repent of harsh words, silent resentment, or withheld affection. Wash me clean, and let my love for her reflect Your sacrificial love for the Church. May my actions say, 'Not my will, but Yours'—and hers—'be done.'

Fill me with Your grace to:

Listen before I speak (James 1:19),

Serve without expecting praise (Philippians 2:4),

Love her as Christ loves me (Ephesians 5:25).

Let humility be the bridge that heals what pride has broken.

In Jesus' name, Amen.

Scripture references: James 4:6, Philippians 2:3-7, James 1:19, Ephesians 5:25
